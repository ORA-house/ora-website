The Mission

We are ORA, a team of multi-disciplinary students from Heriot-Watt University working together to design a sustainable house for the future that resonates with our environment and the lives of the diverse people who live in the UAE.
The UAE is a signatory of the COP21 agreement (http://www.cop21.gov.fr/en) which identifies carbon reduction as a key goal to reduce climate change. We are shaping a future that leverages the expertise of faculty and the enthusiasm of students, eager to put design into practice collaborating with industry partners.

In U.A.E we live with people who are highly cultured and have adapted to the challenges of this environment. It is this environment and our people that inspire our design. The project is like a plant seed that contains both the part and the whole. It will be capable of growth, change, adaptability and resilience to challenging environments, urban, rural locations and the changing needs of U.A.E’s diverse communities.

The Team

The team is composed of an inter-disciplinary mix of students and faculty, with knowledge and expertise to address problems and their innovative solutions from various perspectives.

Our Goal

Our team’s goal is ORA. The aim of the project is to provide the U.A.E with an innovative design for a new type of biomimetic space that sustainably reduces carbon production and mitigates against climate change. ORA will make use of local materials, energy sources and the social and human capital here in the UAE to develop this innovative housing prototype. ORA will provide an exemplary module design adaptive to different users’ lives that reduces greenhouse gas emissions and energy costs, in addition to interacting with the user to offer the best environmental performance aligned to the user’s individual needs.

The Competition

The Solar Decathlon is an international competition created by the U.S. Department of Energy in which universities from all over the world meet to design, build and operate a grid-connected, solar powered house. The houses use solar energy as the only energy source and are equipped with all the technologies that permit maximum energy efficiency. During the final phase of the competition teams assemble their houses in a main expo area, open to the general public, while undergoing the 10 contests of the competition, reason for which this event is called Decathlon. (‘Deca’ derives from Greek and means ten). The first Solar Decathlon was held in 2002 and previous additions have been organized in different areas including the U.S, Europe and China.
